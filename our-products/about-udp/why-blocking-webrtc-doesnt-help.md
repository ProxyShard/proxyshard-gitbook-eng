# Why blocking WebRTC does not protect you from detection

Almost all antidetect browsers on the market simply block <mark style="color:purple;">WebRTC</mark> to prevent leaks. But this is also a red flag. For most regular users, <mark style="color:purple;">WebRTC</mark> works, and if it is disabled, that also looks suspicious.

{% hint style="danger" %}
**The only correct approach is not to block&#x20;**<mark style="color:purple;">**WebRTC**</mark>**, but to proxy it correctly through UDP proxies. For this, both the browser or software and the proxies themselves must support it.**
{% endhint %}
